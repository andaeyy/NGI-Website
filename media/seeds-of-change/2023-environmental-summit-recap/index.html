2024 Environmental Summit Recap

Youth Reconnect with Nature at 2023 Conservation Summit.

Date not listed on original post

By Next-Gen Innovators

4/28/24

The goal of the Youth Summit, an annual event founded in 2022, is to bring youth together to have important discussions about the environment, hear from people who work in environmental fields, get out in nature and, most importantly, inspire our youth! April 27th, 2024 was the date of Next Gen Innovators’ third annual Youth Summit. It centered around mental health, wellness, and sustainability.

This year’s event was hosted at the Tualatin River National Wildlife Refuge, and wouldn’t have been possible without the Friends of the Refuge. We partnered with various organizations to create a fun and activity filled summit for anyone interested in learning more about our environment. Both Sunset and Westview High School’s climate clubs joined us with booths informing others of what they, as clubs, do for our environment. Westview’s booth even offered a paper bracelet-making station for sustainable jewelry!

Other partners included The Xerces Society for Invertebrate Conservation, Five Oaks Museum, Arbonne (an international vegan nutrition company dedicated to creating environmentally sustainable cosmetics and food options), and the EcoMet Club from the Muslim Educational Trust. Every organization had its own booth at the summit, staffed with eager volunteers and employees ready to answer any questions visitors might have.

Apart from the booths, the summit also hosted a sustainable clothing swap where visitors could trade clothes they didn’t want anymore for donated clothing displayed on racks and tables they could browse through. The Youth Summit was concluded by a nature walk led by an employee of the Friends of the Refuge. Visitors explored the expansive refuge, taking in its diverse waterways and lush foliage.
